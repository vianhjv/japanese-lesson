Lesson 9: Conversation - Causative-Passive Form (使役受身の形)

An checks in on Tanaka, who had a tiring weekend after being forced to work by his boss.

An

田中たなかさん、おはようございます。なんだか、とても疲つかれているみたいですけど、大丈夫だいじょうぶですか。

Mr. Tanaka, good morning. You look very tired, are you okay?

Tanaka

ああ、アンさん…。実じつは、昨日きのう、部長ぶちょうにいきなり休日出勤きゅうじつしゅっきんさせられたんですよ。

Oh, An-san... Actually, yesterday, the department head suddenly made me come in to work on my day off.

An

ええっ、日曜日にちようびなのに？大変たいへんでしたね。

What, even on a Sunday? That must have been tough.

Tanaka

本当ほんとうにね。朝早あさはやくから会社かいしゃに来こさせられて、一日中いちにちじゅう、長ながいレポートを書かかされたんだ。

Tell me about it. I was made to come to the office early in the morning, and then I was forced to write a long report all day.

An

うわあ、それは大変たいへん…。

Wow, that's really rough...

Tanaka

それだけじゃない。夜よるはクライアントとの接待せったいがあって、お酒さけが飲のめないのに、無理むりやり飲のまされたんです。

That's not all. At night, there was a client dinner, and even though I can't drink alcohol, I was forced to drink.

An

無理むりやり？ひどいですね。

Forced to drink? That's terrible.

Tanaka

でしょう？本当ほんとうは家いえでゆっくり休やすみたかったのに…。今日きょうはもう仕事しごとをしたくない気分きぶんだよ。

Right? I really just wanted to relax at home... Today, I'm just not in the mood to work.

An

同情どうじょうします…。温あたたかいコーヒーでもいかがですか。

I sympathize with you... How about a warm cup of coffee?

New Vocabulary (新しい言葉)

  • 休日出勤きゅうじつしゅっきん

    Hiragana: きゅうじつしゅっきん

    Type: Noun

    Meaning: Working on a day off

    部長に休日出勤させられました。

    I was made to work on a day off by my boss.

  • いきなり

    Hiragana: いきなり

    Type: Adverb

    Meaning: Suddenly, unexpectedly

    いきなり雨が降ってきた。

    It suddenly started to rain.

  • 一日中いちにちじゅう

    Hiragana: いちにちじゅう

    Type: Noun (Adverbial)

    Meaning: All day long

    昨日は一日中、家にいました。

    I was at home all day yesterday.

  • クライアント

    Hiragana: くらいあんと

    Type: Noun

    Meaning: Client

    これからクライアントと打ち合わせです。

    I have a meeting with a client now.

  • 接待せったい

    Hiragana: せったい

    Type: Noun

    Meaning: Entertaining clients, business dinner

    夜は接待があります。

    There's a client dinner tonight.

  • 無理むりやり

    Hiragana: むりやり

    Type: Adverb

    Meaning: Forcibly, against one's will

    無理やりお酒を飲まされました。

    I was forced to drink alcohol.

  • 気分きぶん

    Hiragana: きぶん

    Type: Noun

    Meaning: Mood, feeling

    今日は仕事をする気分じゃない。

    I'm not in the mood to work today.

  • 同情どうじょうする

    Hiragana: どうじょうする

    Type: Verb (III)

    Meaning: To sympathize

    彼の話に同情しました。

    I sympathized with his story.

Part 4: Grammar Analysis (文法の解説)

1. Nuance Comparison (Important!)

To fully understand, let's compare how the same situation is expressed in two different forms:

Form Example Nuance Who is the focus?
Causative
(使役形)		 部長は私を残業させた。 Objective, simply states the boss's action. The Boss
Causative-Passive
(使役受身形)		 私は部長に残業させられた。 Negative, emphasizes "my" dissatisfaction and feeling of being forced. "My" suffering

2. Conjugation Rules

Detailed Conjugation Table

Group Rule Example
Group I
(Short form)		 Ending u → a + される 書く (kaku) → 書かされる
待つ (matsu) → 待たされる
Group I (Exception) Verbs ending in す (su) MUST use the long form:
Ending su → sa + せられる		 話す (hanasu) → 話させられる
(話さされる is not used as it's hard to pronounce)
Group II Drop ます + させられる 食べる (taberu) → 食べさせられる
やめる (yameru) → やめさせられる
Group III Irregular する (suru) → させられる
来る (kuru) → 来させられる (ko-saserareru)

Part 5: Grammar Practice (練習)

1. How do you conjugate the verb「歌う」(to sing) into the Causative-Passive form?

💡 Explanation: 歌う is a Group I verb. Change the `う` ending to `わ` and add `される`.
(b) is the causative form, (c) is the passive form.

2. How do you conjugate the verb「やめる」(to quit) into the Causative-Passive form?

💡 Explanation: やめる is a Group II verb. Drop `ます` and add `させられる`.
(a) is the potential/passive form, (c) is the causative form.

3. Complete the sentence: 社長に日曜も会社に (来る) → ？

💡 Explanation: 来る (kuru) is a Group III verb, which conjugates to 来させられる. (I was made to come to the company on Sunday by the president.)

4. Complete the sentence: 兄にご飯を (作る) → ？

💡 Explanation: 作る (tsukuru) is a Group I verb. Change `る` → `ら` + `されました`. (I was made to cook by my older brother.)

5. Complete the sentence: 父に家の仕事を (手伝う) → ？

💡 Explanation: 手伝う (tetsudau) is a Group I verb. Change `う` → `わ` + `されました`. (I was made to help with housework by my father.)

6. Complete the sentence: 先輩に、たくさんの資料を (コピーする) → ？

💡 Explanation: する is a Group III verb, which conjugates to させられる. (I was made to copy many documents by my senior.)

7. Complete the sentence: 部長に、たくさんの荷物を (持つ) → ？

💡 Explanation: 持つ (motsu) is a Group I verb. Change `つ` → `た` + `されました`. (I was made to carry a lot of luggage by the department head.)

8. Complete the sentence: 私は母に部屋を (掃除する) → ？

💡 Explanation: 掃除する is a Group III verb, which conjugates to させられる. (I was made to clean my room by my mother.)
